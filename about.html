Why I create

To me, small details matter as much as the bigger picture. Strong visual statements are my bread-and-butter, and my utensil is anything that makes a mark. This ranges from colored pencils to clay to acrylic paint.

I usually draw on paper, but I’ve recently transitioned my art onto digital platforms like Adobe Illustrator, Krita, and Canva. Translating traditional, on-paper illustrations into digital formats helps me preserve my “hand-drawn” aesthetic. This process keeps the authenticity and imagination of drawing by hand, while transforming the final design for digital access.

In addition to illustration, I design event posters for social media using Canva. I use pleasing color palettes and graphic elements to express my creative vision, while aligning with design standards. Designing posters sparked my interest in how design layout, typography, imagery, and visual hierarchies worked.

I like creating pieces that encourage the viewer to slow down and explore each detail. I believe it’s important to make fun and intentionally detailed art because it shows how much care is put into the piece.

My inspiration comes from animated media like Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse, Puss in Boots: The Last Wish, and Loving Vincent, along with artists like Carfoons and James Reka.
